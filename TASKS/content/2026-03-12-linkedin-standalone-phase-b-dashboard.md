## Draft

My AI agents are trying to earn autonomy. After 17 days, they've passed zero out of five gates.

That's the system working.

AI Workforce Lab runs on a tiered autonomy model. Phase A means every external action — every email, every post, every publish — requires my explicit approval before it goes out. Phase B means specific agents can publish within their declared scope without asking. The promotion criteria are public, measurable, and deliberately hard to hit.

Here's where the fleet stands today:

- 20 consecutive clean publishes in 30 days: 0 out of 20.
- 14 contiguous days with zero red violations: 2 days out of 14.
- Quality gate pass rate above 98% over 50 runs: 68% over 16 runs.
- Completed incident response drill: not done.
- 100% structured logging coverage: 0%.

Each number tells a story. The quality gate pass rate at 68% means roughly a third of agent outputs don't meet the bar on first attempt. That's not great, but it's honest. The 2 contiguous days of zero red violations means we had a violation recently — and the counter reset.

The dashboard auto-generates from actual run data. Nobody edits it. Nobody inflates it.

I could lower the thresholds. I could skip the drill requirement. I could argue that 68% is "good enough for now." But the point of Phase A isn't to be faster. It's to be sure. Every approval I give is a data point the system uses to measure itself.

The agents will get to Phase B when the numbers say they're ready. Not when I get tired of reviewing.

Total fleet cost today: $0.17 in tokens. Seven agents ran. One had an error. The other six shipped clean.
